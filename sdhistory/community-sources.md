# Community Sources: Bostonia, El Cajon & East County San Diego History
## Compiled 2026-03-13

These are Facebook groups, Instagram accounts, websites, archives, and forums where people share old photos and tell stories about growing up in El Cajon, Bostonia, and East County San Diego. The treasure troves are marked with stars.

---

## FACEBOOK GROUPS (where the real stories live)

### 1. "You Know You're From El Cajon" / Lakeside All East County Media Group
- **URL:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/671136676784216/
- **Type:** Facebook Group
- **Privacy:** Likely private (requires join request)
- **Content:** Nostalgia posts, old photos, "who remembers..." threads. Posts about growing up in East El Cajon, working at local businesses, memories of Marshal Scotty's, etc.
- **TREASURE TROVE** -- This appears to be the main hub where people share El Cajon memories. Multiple posts reference it as "the You Know You're From El Cajon group." The group URL also appears under the name "Lakeside All East County Media Group," suggesting it covers the broader East County area. Members post old photos and personal stories regularly.
- **Notable post found:** "Memories of growing up in East El Cajon and working at..."

### 2. "You Know You're From Lakeside, El Cajon Valley, East [County]..."
- **URL:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/266106806737091/
- **Type:** Facebook Group
- **Content:** Nostalgia group covering Lakeside, El Cajon Valley, and broader East County. Similar format -- members share memories and photos of growing up in the area.
- **TREASURE TROVE** -- Broader geographic scope means more perspectives on the whole East County experience.

### 3. "You Know You're From Lakeside California 92040 When."
- **URL:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/39517204505/
- **Type:** Facebook Group
- **Content:** Lakeside-specific nostalgia. Relevant because Lakeside and Bostonia are adjacent communities with shared history.

### 4. The Bostonia Ballroom (Facebook Group)
- **URL:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/2924989267716353/
- **Type:** Facebook Group
- **Content:** Stories and memories about the Bostonia Ballroom, which was THE hot spot for western swing music from the 1940s-1960s. Hundreds of posts. People share memories of seeing Hank Williams, Johnny Cash, Patsy Cline, Merle Travis, Willie Nelson, Bob Wills, Jerry Lee Lewis, Roy Orbison, Carl Perkins, Charley Pride, Merle Haggard, and others perform there.
- **TREASURE TROVE** -- This is gold for anyone researching Bostonia's cultural history. The ballroom (built 1932, corner of Bostonia St & Broadway) was known as the "Grand Ole Opry of the Southwest."

### 5. Everything East County San Diego
- **URL:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/EverythingEastCounty/
- **Type:** Facebook Group
- **Content:** General East County community group. Mix of current events and nostalgia. Good for connecting with locals who have family history in the area.

### 6. Vintage San Diego THE GROUP
- **URL:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/VintageSanDiego/
- **Type:** Facebook Group
- **Content:** Vintage San Diego photos and memories. Broader than East County but frequently features El Cajon, Lakeside, and East County content.
- **TREASURE TROVE** -- Large, active community. Good place to ask about specific Bostonia/El Cajon photos.

### 7. El Cajon Valley High School Alumni
- **URL:** https://www.facebook.com/groups/537238413122628/
- **Type:** Facebook Group
- **Content:** Alumni sharing memories, reunion info, old yearbook photos. Many grew up in Bostonia and surrounding neighborhoods.

## WEBSITES & ARCHIVES (deep research sources)

### 24. El Cajon Historical Society Website
- **URL:** https://elcajonhistory.org/
- **Museum:** Knox House Museum, 280 N. Magnolia, El Cajon
- **Content:** Articles by local historians (including Chloris Scott and Del Hood), newsletters dating back decades, PDFs of historical documents, photos.
- **Key pages:**
  - History articles: https://elcajonhistory.org/echsmedia/echs_history.htm
  - Resources: https://elcajonhistory.org/echsmedia/echs_resource.htm
  - "The Lively Arts in El Cajon": https://elcajonhistory.org/echsmedia/echs_livelyartsinelcajon.html
  - Newsletters (PDF archive): e.g., https://elcajonhistory.org/echsmedia/newsletters/HistSocNewsletter_V33-1_2012-01.pdf
- **Research:** Available by appointment with ECHS Archivist, $40/hour for volunteer research.

### 25. City of El Cajon Historic Photos
- **URL:** https://www.elcajon.gov/Home/Components/PhotoAlbum/PhotoAlbum/4/
- **Content:** Official city photo gallery courtesy of the El Cajon Historical Society. Photos organized by era.
- **Also:** El Cajon Stories project: https://www.elcajon.gov/el-cajon-stories-2
- **Also:** Historical Timeline: https://www.elcajon.gov/discover-el-cajon/history/el-cajon-historical-timeline

### 26. San Diego History Center -- Photograph Collection
- **URL:** https://sandiegohistory.org/gallery/
- **Online Collections:** https://sandiegohistory.pastperfectonline.com/
- **Digital Collections:** https://collections.sandiegohistory.org/
- **Content:** Over 2.5 MILLION images. One of the largest regional photography collections in the US. Documents all of San Diego County.
- **Specific El Cajon/Bostonia finds:**
  - Charles Marks Family Photo Album (1900-1915): Includes images of Bostonia vineyard and grape pickers' tents at Bostonia
  - Postcards from El Cajon: http://www.sandiegohistory.org/collection/postcards/elcajon/
  - Postcard Collection guide: https://sandiegohistory.org/collection/photographs/ac094/
  - El Cajon Historical Society records guide: https://sandiegohistory.org/findaid/ms254.html
  - Contact: collections@sandiegohistory.org
- **TREASURE TROVE** -- 2.5 million images. The definitive archive.

### 27. Calisphere (California Digital Library)
- **URL:** https://calisphere.org/
- **San Diego History Center collections:** https://calisphere.org/institution/111/collections/
- **Content:** Free access to digitized artifacts from California libraries, archives, and museums. Includes El Cajon branch library photos, aerial views of El Cajon Main Street, E. F. Mueller Postcard Collection items.

### 28. Dr. Wokuluk's El Cajon Photos
- **URL:** http://www.drwokuluk.com/el_cajon_photos.html
- **Content:** "Historical Photos of Main Street, El Cajon CA." A private collection seeking photos from every decade (1890s-present). Acknowledges El Cajon Historical Society and El Cajon Centennial as sources. Run by Dr. Gary J. Wokuluk, DDS, a local El Cajon dentist.

### 31. San Diego Troubadour -- Bostonia Ballroom Articles
- **URL:** https://sandiegotroubadour.com/
- **Key articles:**
  - "If These Walls Could Talk: Revival of the Bostonia Ballroom": https://sandiegotroubadour.com/if-these-walls-could-talk-revival-of-the-bostonia-ballroom/
  - "A Night Out at the Bostonia Ballroom": https://sandiegotroubadour.com/a-night-out-at-the-bostonia-ballroom/
  - "When Hank Williams Came to Town": https://sandiegotroubadour.com/when-hank-williams-came-to-town/

### 32. Deke Dickerson Photo Archive -- Bostonia Ballroom
- **URL:** https://dekedickerson.com/photo-archives/scotty-broyles-1/
- **Full archive:** https://dekedickerson.com/photo-archives/
- **Content:** Scotty Broyles was a Navy sailor stationed in San Diego in the early 1950s who shot COLOR SLIDES of musicians at the Bostonia Ballroom. These are some of the ONLY known color photographs of certain performers at the venue, including:
  - Merle Travis posing with his Bigsby solidbody guitar (the ONLY known color photo of Travis with this guitar)
  - Smokey Rogers (house band leader) with Joaquin Murphy on Bigsby tripleneck steel guitar
  - Various other country music legends
- **TREASURE TROVE** -- Irreplaceable color photography from the Bostonia Ballroom's golden era.

## OTHER MUSEUMS & HISTORICAL SOCIETIES

### 49. Olaf Wieghorst Museum & Western Heritage Center
- **URL:** https://wieghorstmuseum.org/
- **Location:** 131 Rea Ave, downtown El Cajon
- **Hours:** Fri/Sat/Sun 11am-3pm, FREE admission
- **Content:** Art and historic home of western artist Olaf Wieghorst, who lived in El Cajon for 44 years (1940s-1988). His paintings were owned by Presidents Eisenhower, Nixon, Ford, and Reagan.
