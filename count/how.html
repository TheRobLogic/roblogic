How To Count

Bancroft Inventory

The Basics

Step 1
Open the inventory page on your phone. Tap a section to open it.
Step 2
Enter the count for each item. Your work saves automatically — if your phone goes to sleep, everything's still there when you come back.
Step 3
When you're done, hit SUBMIT at the top. You'll see a confirmation screen — tap TAP OUT to reset for the next person.

The Buttons

Drafts & open bottles — tap to cycle through fill levels:

F full   ½ half   ¼ quarter   K kicked

Tap again to cycle to the next one. Tap past K to clear it.

Number fields — just type the count. How many bottles, cases, whatever.

Sections

  • Draft — Tapped (what's on the line) + Backup (kegs in the walk-in)
  • Bottles & Cans — Bar (behind the bar) + Cases + Loose (in the walk-in)
  • Well Spirits — Well 1 + Well 2 (fill level of each open bottle) + Backup (sealed bottles)
  • Liquor (whiskey, tequila, etc.) — Open (fill level) + Backup (behind bar) + Room (in the liquor room)
  • Soda / Juice / Supplies — Bar + Back
  • Notes — anything you want to tell us. Broken stuff, things we're low on, whatever.

86 Button

See the 86 circle next to each item? Tap it if we don't carry that anymore. The item gets crossed out and we'll remove it from the list.

Adding Items

At the bottom of every section there's a + row. Type the name of something that's not on the list and enter the count. We'll add it next time.

The Process

You Count
Walk the bar, the walk-in, the liquor room. Enter what you see. Use the Notes section for anything weird — broken equipment, stuff that looks off, things we're running low on.
You Submit
When you're done (or done enough), hit Submit. Your count goes straight to Rob.
Rob Reviews
Rob looks at what you sent, compares it to what we've been selling, and figures out what to order. If you 86'd something or added something new, he'll update the list for next time.
List Gets Better
Every count makes the list more accurate. Items you 86 get removed. Items you add get put on permanently. The more you use it, the less work it is.
Tip: You don't have to do the whole thing in one shot. Count what you can, put the phone down, come back later. It remembers where you left off.
