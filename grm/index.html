Existing Walls & Ceiling - FRP Over Drywall

FRP panels mount directly over existing drywall using FRP adhesive + mechanical fasteners (screws into studs behind drywall). Drywall stays in place. Locate existing studs with a stud finder before mounting. Seal all seams and edges with silicone for moisture barrier.

Tip: On existing walls, run a bead of construction adhesive AND screw through to studs. Adhesive alone may not hold long-term at 90%+ humidity if the drywall paper softens. Mechanical fasteners are insurance.

Notes for Contractor

Purpose: Controlled-environment mushroom growing room. Runs at 85-95% relative humidity continuously. Every seam, joint, and penetration must be sealed. Think commercial kitchen or walk-in cooler, not bedroom.

Existing walls: Leave drywall in place. FRP goes directly over it with adhesive + screws into studs. Locate studs with stud finder before mounting. The drywall paper may soften over time at high humidity — mechanical fasteners into studs are critical, don't rely on adhesive alone.

New walls: Standard 2x4 framing, 16" OC. Insulate cavities with rigid foam, then FRP over studs.

Ceiling: FRP panels screwed directly to existing ceiling joists/rafters through drywall. Same approach as existing walls.

Floor: Existing slab. Flooring finish will be added separately after framing is complete.

Door: Prehung exterior-grade, 36", with weatherstripping. Confirm rough opening dimensions from actual door before cutting.

Electrical (separate scope): Will need 1-2 dedicated 20A GFCI circuits. Outlets mounted high (4 ft+). Not part of framing scope but leave access for electrician.

No drywall on new walls. FRP is the finish surface. No paint anywhere inside.

Penetrations: Do NOT cut vent holes until locations are confirmed. Frame the walls, then we will mark penetration locations before FRP goes on.

Anchor bottom plates: If on concrete slab, use Tapcon concrete screws or powder-actuated fasteners every 24-32 inches.

Assembly order: Frame new walls → stand and anchor to slab → tie to existing walls at corners → insulate new wall cavities → install FRP on ALL surfaces (new walls, existing walls, ceiling) → seal everything with silicone.
