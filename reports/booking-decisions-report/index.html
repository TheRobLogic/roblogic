Typical Bill Patterns

  1. Standard 3--4 band bill (most common for Fri/Sat): e.g., "Go Scarlet / Velvet Glow / Fancy Faces" or "Kissing Candice / Dead Things / Mood of a Sinner / Vertical Patrols / G://Anon"
  2. Metal/punk fest format (5--7 bands): e.g., "Putrid Invocation / Naipes / Cemetery Rot / Bourn Ultra / Pagan Ritual / Demiseria" or "Rock Invasion Fest II: Coatl / Corpus / Age of Darkness / RabbiA / Mexico Cabron / Kid in the Pit"
  3. Two-band bill (often a headliner + opener for ticketed shows): e.g., "Born to Run / Cut Loose" or "Bloodbelly Blues / DeadBrokeDown"
  4. Recurring residencies (single name on calendar): DarkWave Garden (every Thursday Oct--Dec), Vinyl Knights / Daygo Pong (Mondays), Dart Tournament (Sundays)
  5. Curated/hosted events: "Red Room Presents: Red Shades of Dance", "Chronicle 3 Hosted by Tha Speakerboxx", "whatever68 radio festival"
  6. Community events: "Bancroft Gear Swap & Open Jam", "Viet Fest", "Hoots Memorial"

Headliner/Opener Pattern

The Bancroft does not operate on a strict headliner/opener hierarchy for most shows. Bills are typically peer-level local bands sharing a night. Exceptions:

  • Touring acts are billed first (e.g., "The Bunny The Bear / Goat vs Fish", "Angry Samoans / Big Attitude / TBA", "Dayglo Abortions")
  • Fest-format shows with a named event (e.g., "Rock Invasion Fest II") may have a curated running order
  • Ticketed headliner shows via TickPick sometimes have a clear draw act

These signals in a booking inquiry predict it will NOT be booked:

Automatic Rejection Signals

Red Flag Example Why
Agency mass-blast White Mountain Agency, Panda Artist, AFM Entertainment These are copy-paste blasts to hundreds of venues. The acts are typically too big, wrong genre, or both.
Indie On The Move form letter "Booking [BAND] [Day], [Date] at The Bancroft Bar" Low conversion platform. Most acts are genre mismatches or out-of-market.
Act too big for 100-cap Dave East, CupcakKe, G Herbo, Boosie, Waka Flocka These acts draw 500--5000+. The Bancroft is 100-cap. Absurd mismatch.
Genre mismatch Country at a punk bar; jazz trio at a dive bar See genre fit section above
Tribute/cover band "The Ultimate DEF LEPPARD Concert Experience" Not the venue's identity
No music links and no local connection Cold email with no Bandcamp/Spotify/YouTube If the booker can't hear you, you won't get booked
Asking for guarantee/fee "Our rate is $X" or "We require a minimum guarantee" The Bancroft operates on door split, not guarantees. Acts demanding payment are typically agency-routed.
International cold pitch SKW from Milan, Italy (13 emails, never booked) Unless a promoter is routing them, international unknowns don't convert
Confidentiality warnings in email "This message contains confidential information... may result in legal action" Signals agency/corporate booking culture incompatible with DIY venue

10. Operational Rules for the Booking Agent

Booking Pipeline

  1. Booking form submissions get highest priority -- these are from people who took the time to use the official form.
  2. Known promoter emails get second priority -- they bring complete bills.
  3. Direct band emails (to bancroftbar@gmail.com) get evaluated based on genre fit, links, and date specificity.
  4. Platform/agency pitches (Indie On The Move, White Mountain, Panda Artist, AFM) get lowest priority or auto-rejection.

Ticketing

  • The venue uses TickPick for ticketing/guest lists. Most shows have TickPick events created.
  • TickPick processes payments weekly via Tipalti.
  • Guest lists are managed through TickPick.

Show Assembly Rules

  1. A typical Friday or Saturday bill should have 3--4 bands.
  2. Thursday shows can be 2--3 bands or a single residency act.
  3. Touring headliners need 1--2 local openers. The promoter usually provides or the booker fills from the regular rotation.
  4. Metal/punk fests can have 5--7 bands (shorter sets).
  5. Never book over Dart Tournaments (every Sunday).
  6. Vinyl Knights / Daygo Pong Mondays are an ongoing residency -- do not double-book.
  7. DarkWave Garden Thursdays (Oct--Dec) are a recurring residency managed by Bryan Pollard.

Communication

  • Most booking communication happens over email (bancroftbar@gmail.com).
  • Some bookings happen via text (noted in descriptions: "Via text with Nino").
  • Flyers are exchanged via email, usually close to show date.
  • The booking form generates threads with subject "[Bancroft Bar] Band Booking Form - new submission".

Financial Model

  • Door split is the standard arrangement. No guarantees.
  • Door prices range $3--$15, with $5--$10 being typical.
  • TickPick handles online ticket sales; door handles walk-ups.
  • The venue does NOT pay booking fees, guarantees, or appearance fees. Any inquiry mentioning these is likely an agency pitch and should be treated accordingly.
