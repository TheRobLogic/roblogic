Data sources: Toast POS daily sales (2023-2025), Google Calendar show bookings (2014-2026)
Analysis period: Jan 2023 -- May 2025 (871 business days, 303 music show nights)
Key metric: Toast POS Net Sales = bar drink revenue. All door/ticket money goes to bands.

Executive Summary: Key Findings

The numbers that matter

  1. Shows add $505/night -- music shows average $948 vs. $443 without (114% lift)
  2. Guest count is everything -- r=0.94 correlation with revenue. Each guest spends ~$16 regardless of event type
  3. 4 bands is the sweet spot -- $1,106 avg vs. $818 for solo acts (+35%)
  4. Saturday is king -- $1,110 avg show night, 101 shows booked
  5. Revenue is declining -- overall daily avg down 13% over 2 years, but show-night revenue is flat

Who to book

Tier Bands Avg Bar Sales
A-tier (book anytime) Wabash Cannons, Dying Species, Brighton Street Bullies, Grahzny, Ramblin Outlaws $1,500-1,800
B-tier (strong draws) Legions, Club 80's/Klub Therapy, Inciting Riots, Plants, Sideshow, Baked Religion $1,100-1,400
C-tier (reliable filler) Fancy Faces, Matters Most, Lovemask, Close Enough, Discount Cadavers $850-1,050
D-tier (doesn't move needle) Vinyl Knights, New Age Affair, Lights on The Coast, Nonverbal Expression <$650

What to do

  • Book 4-band bills on Fri/Sat -- this is the formula that generates $1,100+ nights
  • Protect the dart tournaments -- they're reliable $605/night on Sundays with zero booking effort
  • Ticketed shows outperform free shows -- the ticket creates commitment and draws a bigger crowd
  • March is the best booking month -- $1,127 avg show night. September/October are weakest
  • Fill the calendar -- every non-show day is a $443 day. Even a mediocre show beats that
  • Address the traffic decline -- overall guests down 15% YoY. Shows are holding but the base is eroding
