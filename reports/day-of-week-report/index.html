Thursday: The Biggest Growth Opportunity

Thursday fill rate has grown from 13% (2022) to 44% (2025), driven largely by DarkWave Garden (12 consecutive Thursdays at avg $704/night). But traditional band shows on Thursdays average $984, with top nights hitting $2,870 (MDC), $2,224 (Sham 69), $2,058 (Thee Rapscallions).

What works on Thursdays:
- Multi-band punk/metal lineups
- Touring acts
- Hip-hop showcases (Admiral Atlas best value is Thursdays)

Target: 36/52 Thursdays filled (69%, up from 44%). Estimated gain: +$5,148/year.

Wednesday: Not Worth Routine Booking

Only $77 average lift. 50% of Wednesday show nights underperform dark nights. Wednesday bookings dropped from 26/year (2022) to 4/year (2025) for good reason.

Recommendation: Accept 4-6 opportunistic bookings per year for touring acts or special events. Do not spend booker effort seeking Wednesday shows.

Monday/Tuesday: Not Booking Days

Monday: $428 lift looks impressive but only 11 shows in 4 years. All were special circumstances (touring acts, memorial shows, holiday). Zero in 2025. Accept the rare touring act but don't seek them.

Tuesday: $268 lift across 14 shows. Same story. Not a real booking day.

Sunday: Underexploited

37 music shows across 2022-2025, averaging $1,298 vs $789 dark-night average. That's a $509 lift.

Several blowout Sundays: $4,557 (Dogface Bastards, Feb 2025), $3,116 (Panther Car, Mar 2024), $2,942 (Black Tie Occasion, Nov 2024).

Shows coexist with the dart tournament. Best format: matinee/early evening shows.

Target: 12-16 Sunday shows per year (2-3/month), especially Oct-Dec. Estimated gain: +$4,072/year.
