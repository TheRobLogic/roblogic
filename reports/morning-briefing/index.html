The Big Picture

The Bancroft did ~$227K in net bar revenue in 2024 (down from ~$250K in 2023). 2025 is tracking similarly or slightly lower. Guest counts dropped 15% from 2023 to 2024. Revenue per guest holds at ~$16.

Shows are the lifeline. Show nights average ~$948 vs ~$443 without — a $505 incremental lift per show night. The path to more revenue is more shows, better shows, and fewer empty nights.

Goal: +30% revenue. Current ~$227K → Target ~$295K = +$68K/year.

That's ambitious. Calendar optimization alone can realistically add $10-15K. The rest comes from booking better-drawing acts, building fuller bills, and not losing bookable inquiries to slow replies.

What the Data Says (verified)

1. Shows Add $505/Night — Fill the Gaps

  • Show nights: ~$948 avg. Non-show: ~$443. Incremental lift: $505.
  • 15 unfilled Friday/Saturday nights in 2025 = ~$7,500 in lost revenue
  • Thursday is only booked 36% of weeks but delivers $355/show lift. Going to 77% = ~$8,500/year
  • Realistic gain from calendar optimization: $10-15K/year

2. 4-Band Bills Are the Sweet Spot

  • 4-band bills average $1,106/night — 35% more than solo acts ($819)
  • Each band brings their own crowd. More bands = more bodies = more drinks.
  • Diminishing returns above 5 bands on a bill.

4. The Response Problem

  • 57% of booking inquiry threads get no reply. But this includes agency spam that SHOULD be ignored.
  • The real problem: even Wix form submissions (our best leads, 23% conversion rate) have a 41% ghost rate
  • Median response time when we DO reply: 2 days. 21% take over a week.
  • Tuesday is reply day — 34% of all outbound goes out on Tuesdays
  • Fix: AI booker triages instantly. Real inquiries get a same-day draft. Spam gets auto-filtered. Nothing bookable falls through.

5. Email IS the Pipeline

  • 85-91% of booked shows have an email trail — email is the main booking channel
  • One band/promoter emails to book the night, then brings the rest of the bill
  • The Wix form is the highest-converting channel (23%)
  • Direct cold emails convert at ~16%
  • Indie on the Move: 2%. Agencies: 0%.

6. Agency Spam is 40%+ of Inbound — 0% Converts

  • White Mountain, Panda Artist, AFM, Universal Attractions — mass-blasting venues
  • Rich The Kid, Waka Flocka Flame, Dave East, Banda El Recodo pitched to a 130-cap dive bar
  • Auto-reject. Instant filter. Don't waste a second on these.

7. The Promoter Network

  • Vilos Cohaagen / Admiral Atlas: most active external promoter (41 emails, 10+ threads, 5 shows in 2025)
  • Jeff Clark: heavy calendar coordination (15+ emails/month)
  • FractoFlow Productions, Stay Strange SD, Superkool Booking: external promoters who build bills
  • Churned high-earners: Knikki Royster ($2,983 avg), Broke Bastards ($1,959 avg), Club 80s ($1,524 avg) — stopped booking in 2024. Worth re-engaging. (Dying Species dissolved — do not re-engage.)
  • The AI booker needs a VIP list — known promoters get priority and faster responses.

8. $16/Guest — It's a Bodies Game

  • Average spend per guest: $15-16 (stable across years)
  • But guest counts dropped 15% from 2023 to 2024 (16,654 → 14,112)
  • Revenue decline is a FOOT TRAFFIC problem, not a spending problem
  • Every person through the door = ~$16 in bar sales. Period.

9. Ticketed Shows Likely Draw Better — But It's Selection Bias

  • Ticketed shows average $1,084 vs $917 for free (+18%)
  • But the shows worth ticketing are already expected to draw well
  • Still worth defaulting to ticketed — advance sales create commitment and give you a headcount prediction

10. Dart Tournaments Are Free Money

  • $605/Sunday average, zero booking effort
  • 89 of 93 Sundays in the data
  • Don't touch this. It works.

11. The Blues Jam Is Losing Money

  • Calendar strategy analysis found the blues jam averages $200-253/night
  • A dark Wednesday averages $432
  • The blues jam is actively costing the bar ~$200/week vs doing nothing

Booker Phase 2 — What to Build

Immediate (Week 1-2): Email Triage Bot

  • Monitor bancroftbar@gmail.com
  • Auto-classify: booking inquiry / follow-up / agency spam / ops noise
  • Auto-reject known spam senders (White Mountain, Panda, AFM, Universal, etc.)
  • Draft reply for real inquiries → Rob reviews → send
  • Auto-attach Artist Info doc on confirmed bookings
  • Flag calendar gaps ("You have no show booked for Saturday April 12")

Short-term (Month 1): Pipeline Tracker

  • Every inquiry: new → responded → negotiating → booked → advanced → played
  • Dashboard: open nights, pending inquiries, stale threads
  • Weekly digest: "3 open Saturdays in the next 6 weeks. 5 bands asked for dates in that range."
  • VIP list: known promoters get flagged for priority response

Medium-term (Month 2-3): Smart Booking

  • Band scoring: genre fit + local signal + play history + revenue correlation
  • Bill builder: "Dying Species is booked Oct 18 — here are 3 local openers who inquired and fit"
  • Proactive outreach: contact bands from the outreach target list when gaps appear
  • Post-show tracking: actual revenue vs predicted, build the model over time

Longer-term: Full Booking Agent

  • Automated offer sheet generation from template
  • Calendar optimization engine (suggest optimal show frequency per month)
  • Seasonal pre-booking (lock down January shows in October)
  • Revenue forecasting per show
