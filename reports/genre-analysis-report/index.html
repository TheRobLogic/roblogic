Interpretation

The conversion rates look low across the board because many "inquiries" are multi-message threads (Wix form -> reply -> counter-reply) counted per email, not per unique band. The relative ratios are what matter.

Clearly wrong venue: jazz, comedy, mainstream country, ska/reggae -- these genres send inquiries but almost never book. They represent wasted inbox time.

Core genres with high inquiry volume: punk, metal, goth/darkwave, rock -- high inquiry counts are expected because these are the genres the venue attracts. Low conversion reflects selectivity (many bands apply, few get booked), not poor fit.

8. Recommendations -- Path to +30% Revenue

The Math

Current avg net on show dates: $1070. Target (+30%): $1391.
Gap: $321/night. Here's how genre strategy closes it:

Tier 1: High-Impact Actions (est. +15-20%)

1. Revive a monthly 80s/retro dance night (Friday)
- Club 80's was the #1 revenue night: $1,529 avg (+43% vs baseline)
- Stopped April 2024 -- that's 20+ months of lost revenue
- Find a new DJ or rebrand. "Club 80's" had brand equity; consider keeping the name
- Target: 1x/month on Fridays
- Est. impact: +$500-800 per event above baseline = +$6K-10K/year

2. Increase punk Friday bookings to 2-3x/month
- Punk on Fridays = $1,611 avg -- the strongest genre-day combo in the data
- Currently only ~0.6/month. The audience exists; the supply is there (513 punk inquiries in 2024-25)
- Est. impact: +$8K-15K/year

3. Add 1 metal show per month (Saturday preferred)
- Metal Saturdays = $1,398 avg (5 shows). Metal is rising in bookings (3 --> 9 period-over-period)
- Saturday scheduling maximizes bar revenue
- Est. impact: +$3K-5K/year

Tier 2: Tactical Experiments (est. +5-10%)

4. Quarterly hip-hop/rap showcase
- Only 5 shows on record but $1,315 avg. MC Chris ($2,107) and Thee Rapscallions ($1,943) both crushed
- Quarterly event with local acts + touring headliner
- Est. impact: +$1K-3K/year

5. Spectacle events (1/quarter)
- Rock'n Wrestling ($3,346), costume contests, themed events all overperform
- Format matters more than genre for these -- it's about the event draw
- Est. impact: +$2K-4K/year

6. Evaluate and optimize DarkWave Garden
- If it beats the Thursday baseline, keep it -- it's serving its night well
- If not, consider reducing to biweekly or moving to a busier night to test
- Measure: compare Thursday revenue on DWG nights vs non-DWG Thursdays

Tier 3: Efficiency / Savings (est. +3-5%)

7. Auto-filter booking inbox
- Auto-reject: jazz, comedy, mainstream country/pop, ska/reggae
- Based on email analysis, this eliminates ~200+ wasted inquiry interactions per year
- Time saved goes to pursuing high-value punk/metal/darkwave bookings
- Add genre dropdown to Wix booking form with preferred genres listed first

8. Cut any new recurring night that can't beat its day-of-week baseline after 6 months
- Open Blues Jam ($382 avg) was correctly discontinued
- Apply the same standard to future experiments

Tier 4: Strategic Positioning

9. Explicit genre branding
- Position as "San Diego's home for punk, metal, darkwave, and weird shit"
- This attracts right-fit inquiries and repels wrong ones
- Update website, Wix form, social media bios

10. Genre-segmented promotion
- Build separate lists/channels for punk, metal, goth/darkwave, 80s/dance audiences
- Targeted promo = higher turnout = higher bar revenue per show
- A punk fan's email list =/= a darkwave fan's list
