Key Findings Summary

The Data Says

  1. Facebook events were abandoned in 2020 and never came back. Peak was 150 events in 2018, down to 1-3 per year since 2022. This is the biggest social media gap — a free promotion tool that's been completely unused.

  2. Only 30% of shows got FB events even during the active years. 867 shows in the FB date range had no social promotion at all.

  3. Instagram posting doesn't drive same-day revenue (r=-0.10). The correlation is essentially zero. IG is a brand/awareness channel, not a direct response tool.

  4. 293 bands from social media aren't in our directory. These played the venue (confirmed by FB events or IG show posts) but don't exist in the booking CRM.

  5. The highest-engaged FB events were headliner shows (SHAM69, Hillbilly Moon Explosion, Nekromantix) — events that would have drawn well regardless of social media. FB engagement reflects demand, it doesn't create it.

  6. Monthly posting frequency doesn't predict revenue (r=0.17). Months with 10+ posts don't meaningfully outperform months with fewer posts.

Actionable Recommendations

  1. Resume creating FB events for every show. It's free, takes 5 minutes, and provides discovery. Even with low engagement, it signals to Facebook's algorithm that the page is active. The 2017-2019 era shows that it's sustainable at 150/year.

  2. Don't use social metrics to predict headcount or revenue. The data is clear — there's no meaningful correlation. Keep using play history, promoter track record, and genre fit for booking decisions.

  3. Add the 293 missing bands to the CRM. These are real acts extracted from FB events and IG show posts that aren't in the master band directory.

  4. Post show announcements 2-3 days before, not day-of. Most IG posts go up same-day. Earlier posting gives people time to plan.

  5. Use IG for brand building, not direct response. The data confirms IG builds awareness (avg reach 91 per post) but doesn't drive immediate bar visits. Focus content on lifestyle and atmosphere, not just flyer reposts.

  6. Track FB event creation in the booking pipeline. When Phase 2 builds the pipeline tracker, add a 'FB event created?' checkbox. Goal: 100% coverage for music shows.
