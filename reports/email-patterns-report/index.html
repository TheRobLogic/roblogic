Email Content Patterns — What the Messages Tell Us

Generated: March 5, 2026
Data: 9,909 emails (2024-2026), cross-referenced with 5,745 calendar events and Toast POS data

What Makes a Good Pitch (Pitches That Led to Shows)

A. Specific date, flexible range

Successful touring pitches name a target date or narrow window.

"I'm doing the San Diego date of the West Coast tour for Obsidian Tongue from Portland Maine in September and I'm looking for holds on 9/27 if available."

One date, one sentence, links to Bandcamp and socials. Booked.

Ask Carol (Norway) asked for Nov 7th specifically: "Sorry for the last minute request, we are currently out on our US Tour, and recently had this date open up" — honest about timing, included Instagram (98k followers), TikTok (104k), streaming links, and a live video from their previous SD show. Booked within 24 hours despite only 20 days notice.

B. Local bands who bring their own bill are gold

Fungal Mirage submitted via the form, then organized the entire Aug 29 show: "There will be 4 bands with 45 min set times. The bands are Cotton Brain, Close Enough, The Paw and Fungal Mirage. We are coordinating a backline." This band appears 9 times on the calendar. They do the work.

Rattlesnake Suitcase (Arizona) built a 3-band bill including local openers, decided set times, cover ($10), and sent the flyer — all before the venue had to do anything.

C. Prior relationship or name-drop matters

"I played Bancroft Bar last Summer, with Samhears. I will be able to put together a good bill, and get some folks out."

Short, references previous show, offers to do the bill-building.

"We have a 3 band lineup ready for a show on April 25 and would love to have the show at the Bancroft. Our band is Silent Vice, we have played with you guys before."

Lineup done, date specific, reference to past play.

D. Music links matter — Spotify/Bandcamp win

90% of form submissions include a music link. The ones that get responses fastest have Spotify or Bandcamp links that can be clicked and listened to in 30 seconds. The IOTM encoded URLs are unclickable garbage.

What Info Bands Provide

From 293 booking form submissions:
- 90% include a music link
- 82% write something in the "Anything else" field
- Only 24% mention a specific date

In email-based pitches:
- 99 include Spotify links
- 32 include Bandcamp links
- 71 mention draw/crowd/followers
- 53 offer a full bill (multiple bands)
- Only 7 mention door split terms

Professional bookers (Happy Nomad, Adversarial Productions, Blind Anxiety Entertainment) always include: genre, comparable artists, touring status, music links, and specific date ranges. Most DIY bands send "hey can we play?" with an Instagram link.

Touring vs Local Patterns

Local bands dominate. Of 218 shows in 2024-2025, only ~22 had detectable touring acts. The Bancroft's bread and butter is the SD scene.

Touring band lead time is all over the map:
- Nocturnal Nightmares Tour (LA): 4.3 months ahead
- Obsidian Tongue (Maine): 3 months
- Jet Fuel Soda (Philly): 5.5 months
- Ask Carol (Norway): 20 days (apologized for "last minute")

Local band lead time is shorter:
- Silent Vice: 6 weeks
- Jordi Hereu (Rockabilly Shakeout): asks ~3 months ahead
- Fungal Mirage: often booked within 2-3 weeks

Touring bands typically need help finding local openers. This is explicit in calendar notes: "IRVING BOOKS $15 Tour package. Will email us. 3 bands. need to find locals." Seven shows explicitly flag "need to find locals."

Standard Bancroft question after a touring band confirms: "Will you need help finding locals to fill the rest of the bill?"

Date Negotiation Patterns

First-choice date works more often than not. When a band asks for a specific date and it's available, Bancroft holds it immediately. Standard reply: "I have you guys on our calendar for [date]."

When dates don't work, alternatives offered:

"I have a couple shows in February and March that I need to find bands for, would you be interested in jumping on a bill or did you want to build your own bill?"

"We have open weekend availability for June-August, which weekend date works best for you?"

The "hold" system: Bancroft puts holds on dates before full confirmation: "I put a hold on that date for you guys." Gives bands time to finalize lineups.

Date changes happen. Jet Fuel Soda moved from Jul 3 to Jul 4 ("Super sorry to do this, but there is there anyway we can move the show to 7/4! Just realized we double booked that day."). Accommodated.

Bill-Building Patterns

This is the core operational pattern at Bancroft. Two models:

Model A: Band builds their own bill (preferred)

Bancroft asks: "Did you want to set up a bill of your own or jump on an existing bill?" — this phrase appears in 23+ emails.

Most successful recurring acts (Fungal Mirage, Fancy Faces, Brighton Street Bullies, Thee Rapscallions) build their own bills regularly.

Bloodbelly Blues offered organically: "I remembered a great Outlaw Country band called The Rollin Ramblers out of Los Angeles." Bancroft: "Yeah see if they can play, that would be awesome."

Model B: Bancroft slots bands into existing bills

For touring acts or bands who don't know the local scene. To Super Weapon: "Let me reach out to Truth in Captivity and see if they would be onboard to getting on a bill with you guys."

Fancy Faces is the go-to local opener. They appear on 13 shows in 2024-2025, often added as support. Other frequent fill-ins: Admiral Atlas (9), Brighton Street Bullies (8), Close Enough (8), Legions (5).

Red Flags in Pitches (Never-Booked Patterns)

A. Agency mass-blasts (40%+ of inbound, 0% converts)

White Mountain Agency (Kublai Kwon): 109 emails across 2024-2025 pitching hip-hop artists on rotation. Identical template every time. Zero bookings. Auto-reject.

B. Indie on the Move platform spam

94 IOTM-originated inquiries, low hit rate. Worst offenders: country/Nashville acts pitching a punk venue. "Nashville Country Music Star... My trio rate is $1200 per performance." Complete genre mismatch.

C. Genre mismatch

Jazz/R&B/country/pop acts pitching a punk/metal/rock dive bar. The Deep End Productions pitching Grammy-winning jazz vocalists is a total miss.

D. No music link, no date, no effort

Form submissions like: "Band Name: Fuck Fentanyl benefit show / Contact: Luis / Send us a link to your music: NA" — goes nowhere.

E. Demands guarantees or payment

The Bancroft model is 100% of door to the bands, no guarantees. Any pitch mentioning rates ($1200-$3500) is DOA.

Implications for the AI Booker

  1. Auto-classify by pitch quality: Specific date + music link + full bill = priority response. "Hey can we play?" with no details = template reply asking for info.
  2. Match touring acts with local openers automatically: Maintain a "available openers" pool from bands who've said they want to jump on bills.
  3. Track band status: Name changes, breakups, moves — the booker needs a living CRM, not a static list.
  4. Seasonal outreach: Hit bands hard in December/January for Q1-Q2 bookings. August for fall bookings.
  5. The hold system works: Keep it. Bands expect a hold before full confirmation.
