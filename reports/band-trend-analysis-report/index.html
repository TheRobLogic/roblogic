Critical Correction: Revenue Attribution

Key Takeaways for the Booker

  1. Protect the Club Lemon/Zak relationship. He brings legacy punk headliners that consistently crack the top 10. Give him priority dates.
  2. Legacy punk headliners are the #1 revenue driver. Angry Samoans, DI, MDC, Dr. Know — these acts command $15-25 tickets and pack the room. Actively seek more of these bookings.
  3. BSB, Deadly Dispute, Big Attitude are support acts, not draws. Stop treating them as headliners. They're great for filling bills but don't generate revenue on their own.
  4. Community/cause events are revenue gold. Memorial shows, fundraisers, birthday fests draw through emotion. Encourage and support these.
  5. Festival-format shows work. Multi-band festivals with low cover ($5) and branding (Whatever68, VietFest, Rock'n Wrestling) consistently draw.
  6. Fancy Faces is overexposed. -47% decline, 15 shows. The go-to "add to any bill" band has been added too many times.
  7. Revive Club 80s. It's on 2 of the top 30 nights WITH NO LIVE BANDS. Just darts + Club 80s DJ night did $2,917 and $2,581.
  8. Bands that build their own bills (Cotton Brain, Woke Up on a Throne, Matters Most, Fungal Mirage) are more valuable than bands that just play — they do the booking work for you.
