Bancroft Bar: Calendar Strategy Report

Analysis Period: January 2022 -- February 2026 (1,442 days of Toast sales data, 617 music shows, 200+ dart tournaments)
Baseline Revenue: $295,325/year (2023-2024 average)
Target Revenue: $346,000/year (+30% from ~$266K historical average)
Revenue Gap: $50,675/year

Data Notes

  • 2022 is incomplete (missing most of Jan-Feb) and was a ramp-up year ($220K). Used for trend analysis, not baseline.
  • 2023 ($298,607) and 2024 ($292,043) are the cleanest full years. These form the baseline.
  • 2025 is missing most of September (3 of 30 days recorded). Adjusted annual would be ~$286K.
  • Sunday "shows" are overwhelmingly dart tournaments (177 of 227 Sunday events), not music. This report separates them.
  • All revenue figures are Net Sales (after discounts, excludes tips).

6. Consecutive Show Fatigue

Saturday: Does a Multi-Night Streak Hurt?

Configuration Avg Net Sales Sample
Standalone Saturday (no Thu/Fri show) $1,088 23
Saturday after Friday show only $1,308 76

No fatigue effect on Saturday. In fact, Saturday does BETTER after a Friday show (+20%). The data suggests momentum: a Friday show may prime the audience, and Saturday benefits from word-of-mouth or overlap crowds. Three-night streaks (Thu-Fri-Sat) perform essentially the same as Fri-Sat pairs.

Summary: The $346K Playbook

  1. Thursday is the #1 opportunity. Go from booking 36% of Thursdays to 77%. This alone adds ~$8,500/year.

  2. Never leave Friday or Saturday dark. Fill the remaining gaps. Combined: +$10,500/year.

  3. Kill the blues jam. It performs worse than a dark night. Replace with nothing or an occasional booked show.

  4. Keep darts on Sunday. They're a reliable $877/night draw that requires zero booking effort. Add one music show/month on top.

  5. Run karaoke on Tuesday 2x/month. Free revenue, no band costs. +$190/night.

  6. Book 13-15 music shows per month. Below 13 leaves money on the table. Above 16 shows diminishing returns.

  7. Don't chase holidays. Only NYE and St Patrick's Day justify premium booking. July 4th and Halloween are money losers.

  8. Lean into Jan-Feb. These months have the highest show-night lift percentage. A $200 show on a January Thursday generates more marginal revenue than an expensive act in September.

  9. Accept the September dip. It's the worst month no matter what you do. Book cheap acts, run promotions, focus energy elsewhere.

  10. Grow average check 8-10%. Calendar optimization closes half the gap. The rest comes from getting each customer to spend ~$2 more per visit. Featured cocktails, shot specials during sets, food truck partnerships.

Calendar optimization + 8% check growth = $345K. That's 98% of the way to target.
