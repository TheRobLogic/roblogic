How to use it

Quick walkthrough

Pick your name

First time you open the page, it asks who you are -- Rob or Kayla. This is how the checklist tracks who did what. Your choice is remembered on that device. To switch, click your name in the top right.

Check off tasks

Click the checkbox to mark something done. It moves to the "Done" section at the bottom with your name and the time. If you checked it off by mistake, you can undo it.

Tap a task for details

Click anywhere on a task (not the checkbox) to open the detail view. Briefing-generated tasks have a "Context from briefing" section with the email thread summary, message count, and how stale the lead is. You can also edit notes here -- they auto-save.

Add your own tasks

Type in the "Add a task" box and hit Enter or click + Add. Click "More options" to set priority (High / Medium / Low) and a due date. Manual tasks show up alongside briefing tasks.

High Medium Low

Filter by priority

The pills at the top (All / High / Medium / Low) filter the list. Tap one to focus on just that priority level. The count shows how many tasks are in each category.

Edit or delete

Open a task's detail view and click "Edit" to change the title, priority, or due date. Click "Save" when done. A red "Delete" button appears in edit mode -- this permanently removes the task.

Add notes

Inside the task detail, type in the notes box and click "Add Note". Notes are permanent -- they create a timestamped log of who said what. Use them to track decisions, directions, or anything Kayla and Rob need to remember about this task. Notes show up on the task card too.

Draft an email

Click "Draft Email" to have AI generate a reply based on the task context and any notes you've added. Type direction in the notes box first if you want (like "$100 guarantee" or "offer April 5") -- it'll use that to shape the draft. Hit "Copy" to grab the text and paste it into Gmail. An "Email drafted" note gets added automatically so everyone knows it happened.

Pass on a task

Not interested? Click "Nah" to dismiss a task. It marks it done with a "Passed" note so there's a record, but it's off your active list. Use this for bands or inquiries you don't want to pursue.

Good to know

A few things

Refresh to sync. If you and Rob/Kayla are both using the list at the same time, hit the "Refresh" link to see each other's changes. There's no auto-refresh.

Briefing tasks appear twice daily. The 9am and 9pm briefing runs add new tasks automatically. If a task for the same contact already exists, the briefing won't create a duplicate.

BRIEFING badge. Tasks with a blue "BRIEFING" tag were auto-generated. Manual tasks don't have this badge.

Done section. Click the green "Done" bar at the bottom to expand/collapse completed tasks. Shows who completed each one and when.
